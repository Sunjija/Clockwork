# CLOCKWORK Music Bible

## Canon Policy

- Treat the map, rulebook, story documents, and user decisions as source facts.
- Treat prompt instrumentation, exact tempo, key, meter, and motif pitches as proposals unless already approved.
- Treat every Suno result as a candidate. A title in the library does not make that result canonical.
- Preserve exact region and boss names in titles unless the user requests poetic alternatives.

## Shared Musical Logic

Use a short, recognizable Tique or memory motif once a reference has been approved. Preserve identity through contour, rhythm, or phrase ending rather than forcing identical orchestration everywhere.

Transform the motif by story state:

| State | Treatment |
| --- | --- |
| Damaged awakening | Broken fragments, missing final note, unstable pulse |
| Exploration | Motif hidden in hook, bass, or counterline |
| Safety and companionship | Warmer harmony, humanized timing, lighter register |
| Threat and pursuit | Compressed rhythm, interrupted cadence, lower register |
| Memory recovery | Clear exposed statement with reduced texture |
| Boss conflict | Opposing boss motif interrupts or distorts player motif |
| Progression release | Motif regains range, resolution, or orchestral support |

## Working Palette

Use this as a compatible palette, not a mandatory instrument list:

- Motif and memory: celesta, music box, felt piano, bell-like metal
- Warmth and character: chamber strings, reed organ, bass clarinet, pizzicato cello
- Machinery and infrastructure: prepared piano, tuned pipe hits, pulley or chain rhythm, dry found-metal percussion
- Depth and danger: low cello, contrabass clarinet, bowed metal, restrained sub movement
- Avoid making every cue a variation of ticking clocks. Mechanical identity may come from phrasing, meter, repetition, pressure, or winding acceleration.

## Cross-Cue Continuity

- Derive a boss rhythm from the preceding region when the encounter belongs to that infrastructure.
- After defeating a boss, let the environmental cue return with one restored layer or corrected pulse.
- Use memory cues as the cleanest motif reference; other cues may obscure it.
- Reuse a rhythmic cell or instrument role across adjacent rooms instead of making every room a separate composition.
- Optional exploration areas should generally use stems, reduced arrangements, or motif variants before receiving entirely unrelated themes.
